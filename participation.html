Full-Paper Submission Instructions

The workshop is a half-day event with invited speakers and oral and poster presentations. Papers will be limited to ten pages of Springer LNCS format .
All papers will be reviewed by at least two reviewers with a double blind policy. The review period will be begin after submission and decisions notified by 8th July 2024. Papers will be selected based on relevance, significance and novelty of results, technical merit, and clarity of presentation. Papers accepted for PIPPI will be published within the MICCAI workshop proceeding in a LNCS volume. Submissions should be 10 pages or less including references, in the final LNCS paper template
Please also submit the following license-to-publish agreement alongside your camera-ready submission.

    Submission Page

  • Please submit your work at our PIPPI 2024 Microsoft CMT here.

      • Camera Ready Submission

    • Please submit your camera ready version via https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/PIPPI2024/ till the 31st of July 2024. You can extend your paper up to 12 pages.
    • The publications will be published with Springer - Lecture Notes in Computer Science. Your submission as zip must contain:
    • The final source files (e.g., .tex file, figures, .bib file, images ...)

The corresponding author cannot be changed once the paper and the copyright form have been sent to Springer. The session times (morning or afternoon) and room will be announced closer to the workshop date). A tentative program can be found here.

          • Topics

        • Topics discussed at PIPPI 2024 include, but are not limited to:
        • Image registration, segmentation or classification
        • Image reconstruction
        • Atlas construction
        • Diffusion imaging (including tractography applications)
        • Advanced structural imaging (e.g. advanced DWI or g-ratio analysis)
        • Advanced functional imaging (e.g. network-based analysis)
        • Fetal and/or placental image analysis
        • Cardio-pulmonary image analysis
        • Abdominal imaging
        • Measurement of long-term cognitive outcome
        • Correlation of imaging biomarkers with functional measurements
        • Novel dataset for developing cohort analysis
        • Reviews of advanced imaging technology or software

        Anonymity Guidelines

        The anonymity guidelines require to remove the author and affiliation information from the title page and the paper's header. The authors have to make references to their work anonymously, by citing it in the third person. If the anonymity requirements are not fulfilled, automatic paper rejection is performed.

    • DEADLINE: 24th September 2024

    Best Paper Award

    The best paper will be selected on bases on the quality of the work and presentation at the workshop by the session chairs. The winner will receive a best paper prize and corresponding certificate.
